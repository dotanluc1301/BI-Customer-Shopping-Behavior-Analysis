1. Product Category and Brand Performance
Chart Type: Multi-Dimensional Performance Dashboard
  1a. Performance Matrix - Bubble Chart:
    X-axis: Sales Volume (number of transactions)
    Y-axis: Average Order Value
    Bubble size: Total Revenue
    Color: Product Category/Brand
    Quadrant analysis for strategic positioning
  1b. Performance Trend Over Time - Multi-Line Chart:
    Monthly revenue trends for top categories/brands
    Shows momentum and seasonality
  1c. Market Share Analysis - Treemap or Pie Chart:
    Visual representation of revenue contribution by category/brand
    Quick view of portfolio concentration
  1d. Comprehensive Performance Table: All key metrics in one sortable table
Attributes Needed:
  Product_Category, Product_Brand, Product_Type
  Total_Amount (SUM for revenue)
  Transaction_ID (COUNT for volume)
  Customer_ID (COUNT DISTINCT for reach)
  Date (for trend analysis)
  Ratings (AVG for quality metric)
What to Focus On:
  Portfolio health: Balance of high/low performers
  Growth vs decline: Which products are trending up/down?
  Revenue concentration: Over-reliance on few products = risk
  Strategic positioning: Stars, Cash Cows, Question Marks, Dogs (BCG Matrix)
  Cannibalization: Are some products eating into others' sales?

2. Ratings and Feedback Trends
Chart Type: Multi-Component Analysis Dashboard
  2a. Ratings by Category/Brand - Bar Chart with Trend Indicators:
  - Horizontal bars showing average ratings
  - Color-coded by rating level (red <3.5, yellow 3.5-4.0, green >4.0)
  - Arrow indicators showing quarterly trend
  2b. Quarterly Rating Trend - Line Chart:
  - Multiple lines for different categories/brands
  - Shows rating evolution over 4 quarters
  2c. Rating Distribution - Stacked Bar Chart:
  - Shows distribution of 1-5 star ratings by category
  - Identifies polarizing products (high 5-star and 1-star)
  2d. Ratings-Sales Correlation Analysis - Scatter Plot:
  - X-axis: Average Rating
  - Y-axis: Total Revenue or Sales Volume
  - Shows relationship between satisfaction and sales
Attributes Needed:
  - Ratings (1-5 scale)
  - Product_Category, Product_Brand, Product_Type
  - Date (for quarterly grouping: Q1-Q4)
  - Total_Amount (for correlation analysis)
  - Transaction_ID (for volume correlation)
  - Feedback (text for sentiment analysis)
What to Focus On:
  Quality trends: Are ratings improving or declining?
  Category/brand comparison: Which have highest satisfaction?
  Problem identification: Low or declining ratings = quality issues
  Correlation insight: Do higher ratings drive more sales?
  Feedback themes: What are customers saying?

  Chart Type: Performance Velocity Dashboard
    3a. Sales Velocity by Category/Brand - Horizontal Bar Chart:
    - Bars showing transactions per month (velocity metric)
    - Color-coded: Fast-moving (green), Medium (yellow), Slow (red)
    3b. Sales Velocity Trend - Line Chart:
    - Monthly velocity trends over 12 months
    - Identifies acceleration or deceleration
    3c. Product Movement Classification - Pie/Donut Chart:
    - Segments: Fast-moving, Medium, Slow, Dead stock
    3d. Days to Sell Analysis - Comparative Table:
    - Average days between purchases for each category/brand

    - Transaction_ID (count per time period)
    - Product_Category, Product_Brand, Product_Type
    - Date (for velocity calculations)
    - Total_Amount (for revenue context)

    Sales Velocity: Transactions per month (higher = faster moving)
    Average Days Between Sales: Total days / Number of transactions
    Sales Frequency Score: Relative ranking of movement speed
    Revenue per Day: Daily revenue generation rate
  What to Focus On:
    Fast movers: Products selling frequently (stock adequacy)
    Slow movers: Products selling infrequently (overstock risk, discontinue?)
    Seasonal patterns: Velocity changes by season
    Cash flow impact: Fast movers = better cash conversion
